Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 16 Jun 26

Last Updated: 16 Jun 26

1 INTRODUCTION

Adomantra ("we", "us", "our", "the Company") is a digital marketing and technology agency committed to protecting the privacy, confidentiality, and personal data of our website visitors, clients, prospective clients, business partners, and all individuals whose information we process in the course of delivering our services.

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") explains what personal and business data we collect, why and how we use that data, with whom we share it, how long we retain it, what rights you have over your data, and how to contact us regarding data privacy matters.

This Policy applies to:

  1. All users who visit our website at https://www.adomantra.com
  2. All clients who engage Adomantra for any service
  3. All individuals whose personal data is provided to us by our clients for the purpose of campaign execution, lead generation, or analytics
  4. All job applicants, vendors, and partners

This Policy is compliant with:

  • The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — European Union / UK
  • The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and CPRA — United States
  • The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) — India
  • The Privacy Act 1988 — Australia
  • General international data protection best practices

By accessing our website or engaging our services, you confirm that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of our website and services immediately.

2 INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We collect personal and business data through multiple channels including our website, direct client engagement, email correspondence, service delivery platforms, third-party advertising platforms, and analytics tools.

2.1 PERSONAL INFORMATION

We may collect the following categories of personal information from you:

  • Full name and professional title
  • Business email address and personal email address
  • Mobile number, telephone number, and fax number (where applicable)
  • Residential address or registered business address
  • Login credentials for client portals or project management platforms
  • Government-issued identification where required for KYC or legal compliance
  • Payment information (name on card, billing address) — note: full payment card details are processed exclusively by our PCI-DSS compliant payment processors and are never stored on our servers
  • Signature and date for contract execution (electronic or physical)
  • Communication records including emails, meeting notes, and chat transcripts

2.2 BUSINESS INFORMATION

For B2B clients, SMEs, and enterprise organisations, we may collect:

  • Company name, trade name, and legal entity structure
  • Company registration number, GST/VAT/Tax Identification Number
  • Registered office address and principal place of business
  • Business objectives, key performance indicators (KPIs), and campaign goals
  • Target audience data, customer personas, and market segmentation parameters
  • Website access credentials (CMS, hosting control panels, FTP)
  • Access to advertising accounts (Google Ads, Meta Business Manager, LinkedIn Campaign Manager, etc.)
  • CRM platform access (HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho, etc.)
  • Existing creative assets, brand guidelines, and marketing materials
  • Customer contact lists shared for email marketing or remarketing purposes
  • Business financial data relevant to campaign budget management
  • Competitor analysis data and industry benchmarking information

2.3 MARKETING DATA

In the course of delivering performance marketing, lead generation, and digital advertising services, we may process:

  • Audience segmentation, interest, and behavioural targeting parameters
  • Demographic targeting data (age, gender, location, language)
  • Paid advertising campaign data (impressions, clicks, conversions, ROAS)
  • Lead generation data: names, emails, phone numbers of your prospective customers captured through landing pages or forms we manage
  • A/B testing results and multivariate experiment data
  • Email subscriber lists, open rates, click-through rates, and unsubscribe data
  • Retargeting pixel data and custom audience lists
  • Search query reports and keyword performance data
  • Social media engagement metrics and audience insights
  • Conversion funnel data and attribution model outputs
  • Heat maps, session recordings, and scroll depth data (where CRO tools are deployed with your consent)

2.4 TECHNICAL DATA

When you visit our website, we automatically collect the following technical data through cookies, log files, and tracking technologies:

  • IP address and derived approximate geographic location (country, city)
  • Browser type, version, and language settings
  • Operating system and device type (desktop, tablet, mobile)
  • Device identifiers (where applicable)
  • Screen resolution and viewport dimensions
  • Pages visited, time spent on each page, and navigation sequences
  • Referring URLs (the page you came from before visiting our site)
  • Exit pages
  • Session IDs and cookie identifiers
  • Date and time of access
  • Download activity and form interactions
  • Error logs and technical diagnostic data

2.5 INFORMATION FROM THIRD PARTIES

We may receive information about you from third-party sources, including:

  • Business information from publicly available databases and professional networks (e.g., LinkedIn)
  • Data from advertising platforms about campaign reach and audience segments
  • Information from referral partners or resellers
  • Publicly available information you have voluntarily made public

4 LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

We process personal data on the following lawful bases under the GDPR and equivalent frameworks:

4.1 CONTRACT PERFORMANCE (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR)

Processing is necessary to perform a contract with you — for example, to deliver services set out in a signed Statement of Work, to onboard a new client, or to fulfil a purchase order.

4.2 LEGITIMATE INTERESTS (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR)

Processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms. Legitimate interests include: improving and growing our business, fraud prevention, direct marketing to existing clients and prospects, network security, and internal analytics.

4.3 CONSENT (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR)

Where you have given clear, specific, and informed consent — for example, by subscribing to our newsletter, accepting non-essential cookies, or opting in to receive marketing communications from us.

4.4 LEGAL OBLIGATION (Article 6(1)(c) GDPR)

Where processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which we are subject, such as tax laws, anti-money laundering requirements, or responding to lawful requests from government authorities.

4.5 WITHDRAWAL OF CONSENT

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time by contacting us at info@adomantra.com. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

8 INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

As a global digital marketing agency serving clients across India, the United States, the United Kingdom, Australia, and other international markets, we and our third-party service providers may transfer, store, and process personal data in countries other than your country of residence.

We take the following steps to ensure that international data transfers are conducted lawfully and with appropriate safeguards:

  1. Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): We execute SCCs approved by the European Commission with third-party processors located outside the EEA.
  1. Adequacy Decisions: Where the European Commission has issued an adequacy decision for the destination country, we rely on that decision.
  1. UK IDTA: For transfers from the United Kingdom, we use the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or the Addendum to SCCs.
  1. EU-US Data Privacy Framework: Where applicable, we rely on certifications under this Framework for transfers to the United States.
  1. DPDP Act Compliance: For personal data originating from India, we comply with the provisions of the DPDP Act 2023 and any cross-border transfer restrictions or conditions notified by the Central Government.

If you would like further information about the specific safeguards in place for international transfers, please contact us at info@adomantra.com.

9 YOUR DATA RIGHTS

9.1 GDPR RIGHTS — EEA AND UK RESIDENTS

RIGHT OF ACCESS (Article 15): Obtain a copy of all personal data we hold about you and information about how it is being used.

RIGHT TO RECTIFICATION (Article 16): Request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

RIGHT TO ERASURE (Article 17): Request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary, where consent is withdrawn, or where it has been processed unlawfully.

RIGHT TO RESTRICTION OF PROCESSING (Article 18): Request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.

RIGHT TO DATA PORTABILITY (Article 20): Receive a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

RIGHT TO OBJECT (Article 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

RIGHTS RELATED TO AUTOMATED DECISION-MAKING (Article 22): Not be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

RIGHT TO LODGE A COMPLAINT: Lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g., ICO in the UK, or the relevant EU Data Protection Authority).

To exercise any of these rights, submit a written request to info@adomantra.com. We will respond within 30 days of receipt of a valid request.

9.2 CCPA RIGHTS — CALIFORNIA RESIDENTS

RIGHT TO KNOW: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected about you, the sources, the business purposes, and the categories of third parties with whom data is shared.

RIGHT TO DELETE: Request deletion of personal information we have collected about you, subject to certain exceptions.

RIGHT TO CORRECT: Request correction of inaccurate personal information.

RIGHT TO OPT-OUT OF SALE OR SHARING: We do not sell personal information.

RIGHT TO LIMIT USE OF SENSITIVE PERSONAL INFORMATION: Limit the use of sensitive personal information to specific permitted purposes.

RIGHT TO NON-DISCRIMINATION: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights.

To submit a verifiable consumer request, contact us at info@adomantra.com or +91 8800444999. We will respond within 45 days of receipt.

9.3 DPDP RIGHTS — INDIA RESIDENTS

RIGHT TO ACCESS INFORMATION: Obtain a summary of personal data being processed and information about processing activities.

RIGHT TO CORRECTION AND ERASURE: Request correction of inaccurate data and erasure of data no longer necessary for the purpose collected.

RIGHT TO GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL: Lodge grievances with our Data Protection Officer and receive a timely response.

RIGHT TO NOMINATE: Nominate another individual to exercise rights in the event of your death or incapacity.

RIGHT TO CONSENT WITHDRAWAL: Withdraw consent given to us for processing personal data at any time.

Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at info@adomantra.com.

10 DATA SECURITY

Adomantra implements industry-standard technical and organisational security measures to protect personal data against unauthorised or unlawful access, accidental loss, destruction, alteration, or disclosure. Our security measures include:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmitted between your browser and our website and servers
  • Encrypted storage for sensitive data at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for access to internal systems and client accounts
  • Role-based access controls (RBAC) ensuring team members access only the data necessary for their role
  • Regular internal and third-party security audits and penetration testing
  • Patch management and vulnerability scanning
  • Employee security awareness training and confidentiality obligations
  • Incident response plan and data breach notification procedures
  • Use of reputable, security-certified cloud service providers

Despite these measures, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the relevant supervisory authority in accordance with applicable law.
