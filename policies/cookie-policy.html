1 INTRODUCTION

This Cookie Policy explains how Adomantra ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website https://www.adomantra.com. It describes what cookies are, the types of cookies we use, why we use them, and how you can manage your cookie preferences.

By continuing to use our website after reviewing this policy, or by clicking "Accept" on our cookie consent banner, you consent to our use of cookies as described herein.

2 WHAT ARE COOKIES?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites work efficiently, to improve user experience, and to provide reporting information.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, Adomantra) are called "first- party cookies". Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called "third-party cookies". Third-party cookies enable third-party features or functionality to be provided on or through the website (e.g., advertising, interactive content, and analytics).

Cookies may be: SESSION COOKIES: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They are used to remember information as you navigate between pages.

PERSISTENT COOKIES: Cookies that remain on your device for a specified period of time, or until you delete them manually.

Similar technologies we may use include:

WEB BEACONS (also known as "tracking pixels" or "clear gifs"): Tiny images embedded in web pages or emails that track whether a page or email has been accessed.

LOCAL STORAGE AND SESSION STORAGE: Browser-based storage mechanisms used to store small pieces of data on your device.

JAVASCRIPT TAGS: Code snippets embedded in our pages that enable analytics, advertising, and personalisation tools to function.

FINGERPRINTING: In some circumstances, we or our third-party partners may use device fingerprinting to identify your device across sessions.

3 TYPES OF COOKIES WE USE

3.1 ESSENTIAL / STRICTLY NECESSARY COOKIES

These cookies are required for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled. They are set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in to secure areas, or filling in forms.

These cookies do not collect any personally identifiable information about you and they cannot be used to track your browsing activity on other websites.

Examples:

  • Session management cookies (to keep you logged in)
  • Security cookies (to prevent cross-site request forgery attacks)
  • Load-balancing cookies (to ensure website performance)
  • Cookie consent preference cookies (to remember your cookie choices)

Legal basis: Strictly necessary — no consent required.

3.2 PERFORMANCE / FUNCTIONALITY COOKIES

These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make and provide enhanced, more personalised features. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

Examples:

  • Language preference cookies
  • Region or location-based cookies
  • Accessibility preference cookies
  • Cookies that remember form field completions

Legal basis: Consent (where not strictly necessary).

3.3 ANALYTICS COOKIES

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. They allow us to count visits, identify traffic sources, and improve the structure and content of our website.

Examples:

  • Cookies placed by Google Analytics 4
  • Cookies placed by Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity (session recording tools)
  • Internal tracking cookies we operate for site performance analysis

Legal basis: Consent.

3.4 MARKETING AND ADVERTISING COOKIES

Marketing cookies are used to track visitors across websites and display advertising that is relevant and engaging for the individual user. They may be used to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant adverts on other sites. These cookies are set through our website by our advertising partners. They do not store directly personal information but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device.

Legal basis: Consent.

3.5 THIRD-PARTY COOKIES

Some cookies are placed by third parties when you visit our website. We do not control these cookies and they are governed by the respective third party's privacy policy.

5 COOKIE CONSENT MANAGEMENT

5.1 CONSENT BANNER

When you first visit our website, a cookie consent banner will be displayed. Through this banner, you can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject all non-essential cookies
  • Customise your preferences by cookie category

Your preferences will be stored using a consent cookie so that you are not repeatedly asked for the same consent. You may change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in our website footer.

5.2 CHANGING YOUR CONSENT

You can withdraw or modify your cookie consent at any time by:

  1. Clicking the "Cookie Settings" or "Manage Cookies" link in our website footer
  2. Clearing your browser cookies and revisiting our website (this will reset the consent banner)

5.3 IMPACT OF DECLINING COOKIES

Declining non-essential cookies will not affect your ability to browse our website, but may result in:

  • Less relevant advertising when you browse other websites
  • Some personalisation features being unavailable
  • Our inability to understand how you found our website

6. BROWSER-LEVEL CONTROLS

In addition to our Cookie Consent Manager, most web browsers provide controls to manage cookies at the browser level. You can typically view cookies stored on your device, delete individual or all cookies, block cookies from specific websites, block all third-party cookies, and set your browser to notify you when a cookie is set.

Instructions for managing cookies in popular browsers:

Google Chrome chrome://settings/cookies
Mozilla Firefox about:preferences#privacy
Apple Safari Preferences > Privacy
Microsoft Edge edge://settings/privacy
Opera opera://settings/cookies

Please note that disabling cookies through your browser may affect the functionality of many websites, not just ours.
