1. INTRODUCTION

Adomantra ("we", "us", "our", "the Company") is a digital marketing and technology agency committed to protecting the privacy, confidentiality, and personal data of our website visitors, clients, prospective clients, business partners, and all individuals whose information we process in the course of delivering our services.

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") explains:

  • What personal and business data we collect
  • Why and how we use that data
  • With whom we share it
  • How long we retain it
  • What rights you have over your data
  • How to contact us regarding data privacy matters

This Policy applies to:

  • All users who visit our website at adomantra
  • All clients who engage Adomantra for any service
  • All individuals whose personal data is provided to us by our clients for the purpose of campaign execution, lead generation, or analytics
  • All job applicants, vendors, and partners

This Policy is compliant with:

  • The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — European Union / UK
  • The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and CPRA — United States
  • The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) — India
  • The Privacy Act 1988 — Australia
  • General international data protection best practices

By accessing our website or engaging our services, you confirm that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of our website and services immediately.

4.3 CONSENT (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR)

Where you have given clear, specific, and informed consent — for example, by subscribing to our newsletter, accepting nonessential cookies, or opting in to receive marketing communications from us.

4.4 LEGAL OBLIGATION (Article 6(1)(c) GDPR)

Where processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which we are subject, such as tax laws, antimoney laundering requirements, or responding to lawful requests from government authorities.

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time by contacting us at [Email Address]. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

5. COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

We use cookies, web beacons, pixels, JavaScript tags, local storage, and similar tracking technologies on our website to:

  • Enable essential website functionality and security
  • Remember your preferences and session information
  • Measure and analyse website traffic and user behaviour
  • Deliver targeted advertising and remarketing campaigns
  • Improve user experience through personalisation

Types of technologies we use:

  • COOKIES: Small text files stored on your browser or device when you visit our website. Cookies can be sessionbased (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent (remaining for a defined period).
  • PIXELS / WEB BEACONS: Tiny transparent images embedded in web pages or emails that allow us to track opens, clicks, and conversions.
  • JAVASCRIPT TAGS: Code snippets embedded in our website pages that enable analytics, advertising, and personalisation tools to function.

For a comprehensive description of every cookie and tracking technology we deploy, their retention periods, and detailed optout instructions, please refer to our Cookie Policy at Adomantra/cookiepolicy.

You may manage your cookie preferences at any time through our Cookie Consent Manager, accessible via the cookie banner displayed on your first visit to our website.

9. YOUR DATA RIGHTS

9.1 GDPR RIGHTS - EEA AND UK RESIDENTS

If you are located in the European Economic Area, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom, you have the following rights under the GDPR (or UK GDPR):

  • RIGHT OF ACCESS (Article 15): Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you and information about how it is being used.
  • RIGHT TO RECTIFICATION (Article 16): Request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.
  • RIGHT TO ERASURE (Article 17): Request that we delete your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, where consent has been withdrawn, or where it has been processed unlawfully.
  • RIGHT TO RESTRICTION OF PROCESSING (Article 18): Request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances (e.g., while accuracy is contested).
  • RIGHT TO DATA PORTABILITY (Article 20): Receive a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machinereadable format, and request that we transmit that data to another controller.
  • RIGHT TO OBJECT (Article 21): Object to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes or where processing is based on legitimate interests.
  • RIGHTS RELATED TO AUTOMATED DECISIONMAKING AND PROFILING (Article 22): Not be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing (including profiling) that produces legal or similarly significant effects.
  • RIGHT TO LODGE A COMPLAINT: Lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g., ICO in the UK, or the relevant EU Data Protection Authority).

To exercise any of these rights, submit a written request to [Email Address]. We will respond within 30 days of receipt of a valid request.

9.2 CCPA RIGHTS — CALIFORNIA RESIDENTS

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the CPRA grants you the following rights:

  • RIGHT TO KNOW: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected about you, the categories of sources, the business or commercial purposes for collection, and the categories of third parties with whom data is shared.
  • RIGHT TO DELETE: Request deletion of personal information we have collected about you, subject to certain exceptions (e.g., where retention is required by law or to complete a transaction).
  • RIGHT TO CORRECT: Request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • RIGHT TO OPTOUT OF SALE OR SHARING: We do not sell personal information. We do not share personal information for crosscontext behavioural advertising except where consent has been obtained.
  • RIGHT TO LIMIT USE OF SENSITIVE PERSONAL INFORMATION: Limit the use of sensitive personal information to specific permitted purposes.
  • RIGHT TO NONDISCRIMINATION: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights.

To submit a verifiable consumer request, contact us at [Email Address] or [Phone Number]. We will respond within 45 days of receipt.

9.3 DPDP RIGHTS — INDIA RESIDENTS

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (India), data principals have the following rights:

  • RIGHT TO ACCESS INFORMATION ABOUT PERSONAL DATA: Obtain a summary of the personal data being processed and information about processing activities.
  • RIGHT TO CORRECTION AND ERASURE: Request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal data, and request erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.
  • RIGHT TO GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL: Lodge grievances with our designated Data Protection Officer and receive a timely response.
  • RIGHT TO NOMINATE: Nominate another individual to exercise rights under the DPDP Act in the event of your death or incapacity.
  • RIGHT TO CONSENT WITHDRAWAL: Withdraw consent given to us for processing personal data, at any time.

Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at [Email Address].

10. DATA SECURITY

Adomantra implements industrystandard technical and organisational security measures to protect personal data against unauthorised or unlawful access, accidental loss, destruction, alteration, or disclosure.

Our security measures include:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmitted between your browser and our website and servers
  • Encrypted storage for sensitive data at rest
  • Multifactor authentication (MFA) for access to internal systems and client accounts
  • Rolebased access controls (RBAC) ensuring team members access only the data necessary for their role
  • Regular internal and thirdparty security audits and penetration testing
  • Patch management and vulnerability scanning
  • Employee security awareness training and confidentiality obligations
  • Incident response plan and data breach notification procedures
  • Use of reputable, securitycertified cloud service providers (ISO 27001, SOC 2 certified where applicable)
  • Formal data processing agreements with all thirdparty processors

Despite these measures, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your data. In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the relevant supervisory authority in accordance with applicable law.
