Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 16 Jun 26

Last Updated: 16 Jun 26

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between:

DATA CONTROLLER: [Client Company Name] [Client Address] ("Controller" or "Client")

DATA PROCESSOR: Adomantra New Delhi, India ("Processor" or "Agency")

This DPA forms part of and is incorporated into the Master Service Agreement or service contract between the Parties (the "Principal Agreement").

ARTICLE 1 PURPOSE

1.1 This DPA sets out the terms and conditions under which Agency, acting as a data processor, processes personal data on behalf of Client, acting as the data controller, in connection with the Services provided under the Principal Agreement.

1.2 This DPA is intended to ensure that the processing of personal data complies with the requirements of:

  • Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)
  • The UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018
  • The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (India)
  • The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / CPRA
  • Any other applicable data protection legislation

1.3 In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement regarding data protection matters, the terms of this DPA shall prevail.

ARTICLE 2 DEFINITIONS

2.1 "GDPR" means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council (General Data Protection Regulation).

2.2 "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("data subject"), as defined under the GDPR and applicable data protection laws.

2.3 "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, storage, adaptation, retrieval, use, disclosure, erasure, or destruction.

2.4 "Data Controller" or "Controller" means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data — in this DPA, the Client.

2.5 "Data Processor" or "Processor" means the entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller — in this DPA, Adomantra.

2.6 "Data Subject" means the natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

2.7 "Sub-Processor" means any processor engaged by Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

2.8 "Personal Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

2.9 "Standard Contractual Clauses" or "SCCs" means the standard data protection clauses approved by the European Commission for international transfers of personal data.

2.10 "Supervisory Authority" means the competent data protection authority in the relevant jurisdiction.

ARTICLE 3 ROLES OF CONTROLLER AND PROCESSOR

3.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that:

  1. Client is the Data Controller of Personal Data processed in connection with the Services
  2. Agency is the Data Processor, processing Personal Data solely on Client's instructions and on Client's behalf
  3. Agency shall not process Personal Data for its own purposes or for any purpose other than those specified in this DPA and the Principal Agreement

3.2 Client acknowledges that it is responsible for:

  1. Establishing a lawful legal basis for the processing of Personal Data under applicable data protection laws
  2. Ensuring that data subjects have been informed about the processing of their Personal Data in accordance with applicable laws
  3. Complying with its obligations as a Data Controller under applicable data protection laws

ARTICLE 4 NATURE AND PURPOSE OF PROCESSING

4.1 NATURE OF PROCESSING

Agency processes Personal Data in the following contexts:

  • Managing and optimising paid advertising campaigns on behalf of Client
  • Building and managing custom audience segments for retargeting
  • Executing email marketing and automation campaigns
  • Analysing website visitor behaviour and campaign performance
  • Managing Client's social media accounts and content
  • Processing lead generation data captured through forms and landing pages
  • Managing CRM data integration and marketing automation workflows

4.2 PURPOSE OF PROCESSING

Personal Data is processed solely for the purpose of:

  • Delivering the Services described in the Principal Agreement and SOWs
  • Reporting campaign performance to Client
  • Optimising advertising delivery and audience targeting on Client's behalf
  • Fulfilling Client's instructions as documented in SOWs and communications

4.3 DURATION

Processing shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for the duration of the Principal Agreement, unless terminated earlier in accordance with Article 15 of this DPA.

ARTICLE 5 CATEGORIES OF DATA SUBJECTS

The categories of data subjects whose Personal Data is processed under this DPA may include:

  1. Client's existing customers and account holders
  2. Prospective customers and marketing leads
  3. Website visitors tracked through analytics and advertising pixels
  4. Email subscribers on Client's mailing lists
  5. Social media followers and ad audiences
  6. E-commerce platform users and purchasers
  7. B2B contacts and business partners of Client

ARTICLE 7 PROCESSING INSTRUCTIONS

7.1 Agency shall process Personal Data only in accordance with Client's documented instructions. The Principal Agreement, SOWs, and written communications from Client's authorised contacts constitute documented instructions for the purposes of this DPA.

7.2 Agency shall promptly notify Client if, in Agency's opinion, an instruction violates applicable data protection law, without being required to follow such instruction pending Client's response.

7.3 Agency shall not process Personal Data for any purpose other than those specified in this DPA without Client's prior written consent.

7.4 To the extent that Agency is required by applicable law to process Personal Data for purposes other than Client's instructions, Agency shall inform Client of such requirement prior to processing, unless prohibited by law from doing so.

ARTICLE 8 SECURITY MEASURES

8.1 Agency shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect Personal Data against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage, and unauthorised access or disclosure.

8.2 Such measures shall include, at minimum:

  1. Encryption of Personal Data in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest where technically feasible and appropriate
  2. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for access to systems processing Personal Data
  3. Role-based access controls ensuring minimum necessary access
  4. Regular security assessments, vulnerability scanning, and patch management
  5. Documented incident response and breach notification procedures
  6. Employee training on data protection and information security
  7. Physical security controls for any on-premises data storage
  8. Use of reputable, security-certified cloud service providers

8.3 Agency shall periodically review and update security measures to account for evolving threats and technological developments.

8.4 Upon reasonable request, Agency shall provide Client with documentation evidencing the implementation of security measures described in this Article.

ARTICLE 9 CONFIDENTIALITY

9.1 Agency shall ensure that all personnel authorised to process Personal Data under this DPA:

  1. Are subject to a binding duty of confidentiality with respect to Personal Data (either statutory, contractual, or professional)
  2. Have received appropriate training on data protection obligations
  3. Process Personal Data only as necessary for the performance of their duties in delivering the Services

9.2 Agency shall ensure that access to Personal Data is restricted to personnel who need access to perform the contracted Services.

ARTICLE 10 SUB-PROCESSORS

10.1 GENERAL AUTHORISATION

Client hereby provides general authorisation for Agency to engage Sub-Processors to assist in delivering the Services, subject to the requirements of this Article.

10.2 SUB-PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

Before engaging any Sub-Processor to process Personal Data, Agency shall:

  1. Execute a written data processing agreement with the Sub-Processor imposing obligations equivalent to those in this DPA
  2. Conduct appropriate due diligence on the Sub-Processor's security measures and data protection practices

10.3 AGENCY RESPONSIBILITY

Agency shall remain fully liable to Client for the acts and omissions of its Sub-Processors as if Agency had performed the processing directly.
