Enterprise B2B SaaS 12-Month Campaign

Scaling Global Pipeline Demands: How an Omni-Channel SEO & Account-Based Marketing Strategy Delivered 315% ROI

This deep-dive case study analyzes how we overhauled a fragmented digital footprint for a leading enterprise SaaS provider, shifting from low-intent traffic generation to highly qualified enterprise pipeline acquisition.

315% Attributed via multi-touch revenue models
+142% Optimized through behavior scoring
-38% Reduced blended CAC across active channels

Client Overview & Pre-Campaign Baseline

The client is an international B2B software vendor specializing in AI-driven supply chain logistics infrastructure. Operating across North America and EMEA markets, they faced scaling roadblocks despite strong venture funding.

The Initial Technical Status

  • Legacy Subdomains: Content was scattered across three distinct subdomains, fracturing global domain authority.
  • Inefficient Crawl Budget: Massive indexation of duplicate faceted navigation pages wasted 40% of search engine crawl capacity.
  • Tracking Blindspots: No cross-domain attribution tracking was active, resulting in accurate conversions being misattributed to direct traffic.

Pre-Campaign Benchmarks (Q4 Baseline)

Organic Monthly Traffic:
45,000 unique sessions
Average Demo Sign-up Rate:
0.45% baseline conversion
Sales Pipeline Velocity:
68-day average opportunity cycle

The Core Business Challenges

Our baseline discovery period highlighted three distinct systemic challenges that prevented sustainable, organic scale:

01

High Traffic, Zero Pipeline Value

The client's existing blog content targeted ultra-broad, transactional keywords (e.g., "what is logistics"). While this drove high raw impression volumes, it attracted entry-level students and job-seekers rather than enterprise Chief Procurement Officers (CPOs).

02

Surging Ad Spend Inefficiencies

Paid search campaigns utilized broad-match keywords on competitive phrases, causing cost-per-click rates to surge over $45. Retargeting infrastructures were static, serving identical banner creative to users regardless of their position in the buying funnel.

03

Content Silos and Zero Intent Mapping

Marketing collateral was designed without structural alignment to specific stages of the enterprise buyer's journey. Top-of-funnel users were pushed immediately to hard sales demos, killing conversion rates.

The Strategic Road Map & Execution

To address these challenges, we designed a unified, 3-pronged marketing framework executing concurrently across Technical SEO, Bottom-of-Funnel Content Architecture, and Intent-Driven Account-Based Marketing (ABM).

Month 1–3: Architectural Cleanse & Technical SEO Restructure

We consolidated the fragmented subdomains into a clean subfolder architecture (/en/, /de/, /fr/). Canonical tag implementations fixed internal duplicate content errors, and we pruned 1,200 low-performing pages.

Subdomain Migration Core Web Vitals Optimization Schema Markup Deployment

Month 4–7: High-Intent Content Hub & Pillar Engine

We abandoned high-volume informational keywords to focus entirely on Commercial and Transactional intent modifiers. We mapped out comprehensive pillar hubs focused on core industry software integrations and competitor comparisons.

BoFu Content Velocity Competitor Comparison Matrix Expert-Led Copywriting

Month 8–12: Paid Media Restructuring & Intent-Data ABM

We integrated 6Sense intent signals with LinkedIn Campaign Manager to serve dynamic creative layers exclusively to target enterprise accounts showing active, in-market search behavior.

Intent-Signal Targeting Dynamic Creative Optimization Multi-Touch Attribution

Strategic Takeaways for Marketing Executives

  • Intention Over Volume: A 10% drop in overall vanity traffic is acceptable if it is accompanied by a 4x surge in commercial-intent page interactions.
  • Infrastructure Is Foundation: Content optimization yields diminishing returns if search engine crawlers cannot efficiently access, render, and index your application's folder path hierarchy.
  • Unified Data Layering: Merging third-party intent data platforms directly with your active ad platforms stops ad budget waste on completely cold corporate accounts.
